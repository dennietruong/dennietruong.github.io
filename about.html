About Me

As a recent graduate student from Colby College (2024), I am passionate about learning new things through immersive experiences, building connections, and trying new/complex tasks. With an extensive background in research, I offer expertise in data analytics, programming in Python, R, and SQL, statistical analysis, and experience in academic publication. My enthusiasm for sports inspired me to merge my strengths through my roles as an educator and researcher in my position as baseball coach, with the long-term goal of becoming a data analyst for a professional sports team.

Dennie currently serves as a substitute teacher for the Garvey School District and a baseball/volleyball coach for Woodcraft Rangers in partnership with the Los Angeles Dodgers Foundation. In addition, he currently interns at Bigelow Laboratory for Ocean Sciences under senior scientist, Maya Groner, for the marine disease ecology lab.

Previously, he was a NASA Student Airborne Research Program (SARP) intern becoming the inaugural class of SARP East 2023, one of 22 students selected from universities across the United States. During his time at NASA, Dennie flew on NASA research B200 planes measuring aerosols, greenhouse gases, and soil moisture. He collaborated with Susanne Craig, senior scientist in the ocean ecology lab at NASA Goddard, and Gina Ralph, assistant scientist at VIMS, establishing GAM and PCA models using environmental drivers to predict algal growth along the James River.
