NASA Student Airborne Research Program (2023)

Algal bloom is a rapid increase or accumulation of algae in a body of water caused by excess nutrients, usually from agricultural runoff or sewage effluent. The effects of blooms are devastating and can lead to fish kills, adverse health effects on the community, and wildlife mortality. This project explores environmental factors driving algal blooms and evaluates LANDSAT 8 data for characterizing blooms along the James River. I developed models and used LANDSAT imagery to improve environmental monitoring efforts.
