Potential environmental drivers of algal blooms & Utility of LANDSAT characterization of the James River (2023)

Algal bloom is a rapid increase or accumulation of algae in a body of water using caused by excess nutrients usually from agricultural runoff or sewage effluent. Consequently, the effects of blooms are devastating and can lead to fish kills, adverse health effects on the community and wildlife mortality. This project explores the environmental factors that are driving algal bloom and determines the efficacy of LANDSAT 8 data in characterizing such blooms along the James River. I developed models to analyze algal blooms and used LANDSAT imagery to improve environmental monitoring efforts.
